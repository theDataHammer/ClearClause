Why ClearClause exists

The problem with standard supplier contracts.

When you sign up to Microsoft Azure, AWS, or Google Cloud, you're agreeing to their standard terms — a document that is written entirely in the supplier's favour. These terms are long, dense, and deliberately structured to limit the supplier's obligations while expanding yours.

For most organisations, the immediate priority is getting the service running. The contract is secondary. But for regulated industries — banking, healthcare, government, insurance, telco — those contract terms have direct regulatory implications. A gap in a data processing clause isn't just a legal problem. It's a Privacy Act risk, an APRA finding, or a breach notification obligation you didn't know you had.

ClearClause was built to surface those gaps quickly, clearly, and in plain English — and then tell you what to do about them.

Our methodology

How every assessment is built.

1
Public contract sourcing
ClearClause only works with suppliers who publish their standard contract terms publicly — making them accessible, reviewable, and auditable. We use the Microsoft Customer Agreement, AWS Customer Agreement, and Google Cloud Terms of Service as our source documents. When suppliers update their terms, our assessments are updated accordingly.
2
Regulatory framework mapping
Each assessment is mapped against the regulatory framework most relevant to the client's industry. For an APRA-regulated entity, we apply CPS 234 obligations. For a government agency, we apply the ISM. For healthcare, we apply the Privacy Act and My Health Records Act. The framework is set by the client's industry selection and confirmed by the context questions.
3
Context-aware gap analysis
The context questions in Step 3 of the assessment are not cosmetic — they change the output. A client who processes offshore data gets a different gap analysis to one who doesn't. A client requiring ISO 27001 certification from their supplier gets specific clauses about certification obligations. The assessment responds to the client's actual situation.
4
Severity classification
Every gap is classified as Critical (regulatory or legal exposure), Moderate (material contractual risk requiring action), or Advisory (best practice — not required but recommended). Classifications are based on the regulatory framework applied, not generic risk scores.
5
Controls register generation
The controls register is derived directly from the gap analysis and regulatory framework. Mandatory controls are obligations — activities the client must perform to meet their regulatory requirements. Optional controls are best-practice activities that materially reduce supplier risk even when not strictly required. Both include frequency, owner role, and evidence requirements.

Regulatory coverage

The frameworks we assess against.

ClearClause is built for the Australian market and covers the key regulatory frameworks affecting cloud supplier contracts across industries.

Privacy
Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) & Australian Privacy Principles
Governs how personal information is collected, used, and disclosed. Applies to all assessments involving personal data of Australian individuals. Key requirements include data handling, offshore disclosure, breach notification, and data subject rights.
Financial Services
APRA CPS 234 — Information Security
APRA's prudential standard for information security applies to all APRA-regulated entities (banks, insurers, superannuation funds). Mandates specific information security capabilities, third-party (including cloud) oversight, and incident reporting obligations.
Government
Australian Government ISM
The Information Security Manual governs how Australian Government agencies manage information and ICT systems. Includes specific requirements for cloud services, data classification, certification (IRAP), and supply chain risk management.
International
ISO/IEC 27001 — Information Security Management
The international standard for information security management systems. Widely required in supplier contracts across industries. Assessments flag whether supplier terms address certification, audit rights, and Annex A controls relevant to cloud services.
Healthcare
My Health Records Act 2012 & Healthcare Identifiers Act
Governs the handling of My Health Record data and healthcare identifiers. Applies when cloud suppliers process or store health data on behalf of healthcare organisations, imposing strict obligations on access, disclosure, and breach response.
Payments
PCI-DSS v4.0
The Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard applies when suppliers process, store, or transmit cardholder data. Assessments flag whether supplier terms address PCI-DSS scope, responsibility, and compliance evidence obligations.

Our principles

What we believe about contract intelligence.

🔓
Public terms, public accountability
We only assess contracts that suppliers have chosen to make public. This keeps our methodology transparent, auditable, and free from confidentiality constraints.
🎯
Context changes everything
A clause that is acceptable for a retail company may be a critical gap for a bank. We tailor every assessment to the client's industry and specific context — not generic risk scores.
⚖️
Not legal advice — but more than a checklist
ClearClause provides structured intelligence to support better decisions. We are explicit that we are not lawyers and our output does not replace legal review for material contracts.

Contracts change — so should your controls

Our regulatory framework coverage is designed for Australian organisations. Privacy Act, APRA, ISM, IRAP — these are the obligations our clients carry and we take them seriously.
🤝
Tools for advisors, not just end-users
ClearClause is designed to be used by risk advisors, consultants, and procurement professionals delivering structured supplier management to their clients.
